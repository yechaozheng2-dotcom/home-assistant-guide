### Product Overviews

**1. TP-Link Kasa HS110**  
The **TP-Link Kasa HS110** is your best choice for a powerful smart plug that monitors energy consumption. It is very affordable at about **$24.99**, works seamlessly with Home Assistant, and grants you in-depth analytics of your energy usage. This plug can track real-time energy usage, allowing you to adjust your habits to save on electricity bills.

**Pros:**
- Excellent energy monitoring features.
- Easy integration with Home Assistant.
- Good build quality.

**Cons:**
- Requires the Kasa app for setup.
- No direct support for Apple HomeKit.

**2. Amazon Smart Plug**  
While the **Amazon Smart Plug** costs the same as the HS110 at **$24.99**, it doesn't monitor energy usage. It’s designed primarily for easy integration with Alexa but lacks the smart energy features you’ll need for monitoring. It's adequate if you're already embedded in the Amazon ecosystem and don’t require monitoring features.

**Pros:**
- Very easy to set up and use with Alexa.
- High compatibility with various devices.

**Cons:**
- No energy monitoring capabilities.
- Limited to Alexa for smart functions.

**3. Meross Smart Wi-Fi Plug**  
The **Meross Smart Wi-Fi Plug** is a cost-effective alternative at **$19.99** that offers energy monitoring features. It integrates well with Home Assistant but may require a bit more technical savvy for full integration. If you're comfortable setting up custom integrations, this could be a solid choice.

**Pros:**
- Affordable price with energy monitoring.
- Compatible with Google Assistant and Alexa.
- Easy scheduling options.

**Cons:**
- Initial setup can be more complicated.
- Less user-friendly app experience compared to the competition.

### Who This is For
- **Budget-conscious smart home enthusiasts** looking for reliable energy monitoring: The TP-Link Kasa HS110 is both affordable and feature-rich.
- **Home Assistant users**: The Kasa HS110 boasts excellent integration. If you've set up Home Assistant, this plug will fit perfectly into your system.
