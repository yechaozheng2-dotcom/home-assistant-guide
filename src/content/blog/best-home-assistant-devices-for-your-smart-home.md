If you’re diving into the world of home automation using [Home Assistant](/blog/best-home-assistant-integrations-to-supercharge-your-smart-home/), the first step is selecting the right devices. Some devices integrate seamlessly, while others require extra effort or may not work at all. In this guide, I’ll narrow it down to the top contenders, giving you a clear path toward building a reliable smart home setup.

### Bottom Line: Best Overall Home Assistant Devices

- **SmartThings Hub**: Approximately $80. This hub is a powerhouse for integrating a multitude of smart devices. It supports both Zigbee and Z-Wave protocols, making it versatile and widely compatible.
- **Philips Hue Smart Bulbs**: About $15 per bulb. These not only provide excellent lighting options but also work flawlessly with [Home Assistant](/blog/esphome-vs-home-assistant-which-is-right-for-your-smart-home/) for automation scenarios.
- **TP-Link Kasa Smart Plugs**: Around $25. These plugs are simple to set up and provide valuable control over non-smart appliances, with the added bonus of energy monitoring capabilities.

These three devices provide a solid foundation for your [Home Assistant](/blog/matter-vs-home-assistant-which-is-right-for-your-smart-home/) setup, enabling a wide range of automations and integrations.

#### 1. SmartThings Hub

The SmartThings Hub stands out as one of the best home assistant devices you can buy. Priced at around $80, it acts as a central controller that connects with devices across various protocols, namely Zigbee, Z-Wave, and Wi-Fi. This flexibility means you won't be limited to just one brand or type of device.

**Use Case**: If you’re a homeowner looking to create a sophisticated smart home ecosystem, the SmartThings Hub supports thousands of compatible devices, ensuring you have options for everything from lights to security systems.

#### 2. Philips Hue Smart Bulbs

Philips Hue smart bulbs are industry standards for good reason. Costing about $15 each, these bulbs allow for far more than just changing colors. They can be dimmed, scheduled, and automated to match your lifestyle. 

**Use Case**: As a night owl who wants gentle lighting to transition you to sleep, setting your Philips Hue bulbs to dim at 10 PM will improve your nighttime routine significantly.

#### 3. TP-Link Kasa Smart Plugs

For approximately $25, the TP-Link Kasa Smart Plugs are easy to install and enable you to control standard appliances via Home Assistant. More than just remote switches, they offer energy monitoring, giving you insight into how much power you're consuming.

**Use Case**: If you work from home and want to streamline your coffee machine’s operation, integrate it with your smart home setup to start brewing at the same time each morning.

### Other Noteworthy Devices

- **Nest Thermostat**: At $130, its learning capabilities make it one of the best smart thermostats out there, adapting to your schedule while helping you save on energy bills.
- **Ring Video Doorbell**: For $100, this device enhances your home’s security with two-way audio and motion alerts sent directly to your smartphone.

### Who This is For

- **Homeowners Seeking Automation**: If you're ready to automate daily tasks—like adjusting your lights or temperature when you leave for work—these devices are essential.
- **Tech Enthusiasts**: If you're comfortable with setups and configurations, using devices like the SmartThings Hub will allow for an extensive and customizable smart home experience.

### Who Should Skip This

- **Casual Users**: If you’re looking for a plug-and-play experience without the need for an extensive setup, you might feel overwhelmed by the configurations required for some of these devices.
- **Budget-Conscious Shoppers**: While these devices offer great features, they can add up quickly. If you're hesitant to spend significantly on smart devices, consider starting with just one or two of the essentials.

### Making Your Choice

When it comes to building a smart home that runs on Home Assistant, quality matters. You may be tempted by cheaper alternatives, but relying on high-quality, well-supported devices pays off in the long run due to reduced frustration and stable performance.

**Final Recommendation**: Start your home automation project with the SmartThings Hub, Philips Hue bulbs, and TP-Link Kasa plugs. This combination provides a versatile, user-friendly, and cost-effective solution to kickstart your smart home journey.

By prioritizing these devices, you’ll create a fully integrated and scalable smart home solution that can adapt as your needs grow. Don’t skimp on foundational devices — invest wisely and enjoy a genuinely smart living environment.
