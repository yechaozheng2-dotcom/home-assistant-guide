If you're looking to optimize your home energy usage and integrate monitoring into your Home Assistant setup, the [Shelly EM](https://shelly.cloud/products/shelly-em-smart-home-energy-monitor) is your best option. They provide real-time data about energy usage, enabling you to make informed decisions about your energy consumption. Let’s examine some of the top contenders on the market, dive into their features, and see how they stack up against each other.

### Best Overall: Shelly EM

**Why it's the best**: The Shelly EM provides an exceptional mix of performance and integration capabilities at a low price point. Its ability to monitor two circuits simultaneously gives you detailed insights into specific areas of your energy consumption and allows for automation through Home Assistant with ease.

### Why Consider the Sense Home Energy Monitor

**For Advanced Users**: If you want detailed insights into your entire home’s energy consumption and don’t mind spending a bit more, the Sense Home Energy Monitor can be a worthy investment. Its price tag of approximately $299 puts it on the higher end, but its ability to deliver granular data about electronics and appliances usage makes it potential gold for those wanting major energy savings.

### Aeotec Home Energy Meter: For Simplicity

**Affordable and Easy**: At around $50, the Aeotec Home Energy Meter is affordable and user-friendly. If you're new to home automation, you’ll find the setup quite simple. While you won’t have as many features as the Sense, its compatibility with Home Assistant ensures you can integrate it easily into your existing setup.

### Emporia Vue: Expandable Energy Management

**Best for Large Homes**: For larger homes or those needing to monitor multiple circuits, the Emporia Vue, priced at approximately $60, allows monitoring up to 16 circuits with additional sensors. It's highly regarded among energy monitoring solutions, especially for those who want to monitor specific high-power devices.

## Who This Is For

- **Eco-Conscious Homeowners**: If saving on your energy bill while monitoring energy usage is a priority for you, all of these products will provide the insights you need.
- **DIY Tech Enthusiasts**: With rigorous integration capabilities, these devices are perfect for users already engaged in home automation. If you're someone who loves setting up configurations, these monitors will satisfy your penchant for customization.
- **Smart Home Newcomers**: If you’re just starting your journey into smart home tech, devices like the Aeotec Home Energy Meter or Shelly EM are user-friendly options that won’t overwhelm you.

## Who Should Skip This

- **Budget-Conscious Buyers**: If you are looking for budget options without smart integration features, these energy monitors may not be necessary.
- **Casual Users**: If you only want a basic reading of your energy usage without detailed insights, a simpler, less expensive kilowatt meter may serve your needs just fine.

## Important Features to Consider

When choosing your home energy monitor, keep in mind the features that are essential for your needs:

1. **Integration Compatibility**: Ensure it works well with Home Assistant without extensive configuration. Most options on the list have proven to be integration-friendly.
   
2. **Monitoring Depth**: If you want detailed analytics, you might lean toward the Sense or Emporia Vue. For general monitoring tasks, the Shelly EM suffices.

3. **User Interface**: A simple and intuitive app can enhance your experience. The Shelly and Emporia both have well-designed interfaces for real-time tracking.

4. **Expandability**: If you think you might add more devices or circuits down the line, consider a model like the Emporia Vue that allows for expansion.

## Bottom Line

Overall, the **Shelly EM** stands out as the best home energy monitor for Home Assistant users, offering a great balance of features and price. If your household has an extensive energy consumption profile and you’re willing to invest more for detailed insights, the **Sense** monitor provides an advanced solution. The **Aeotec** is perfect for those just dipping their toes into smart energy management, while **Emporia** is ideal for anyone needing to monitor multiple circuits effectively.

Whichever model you choose, you’re making a solid investment in smarter, more efficient energy use.
